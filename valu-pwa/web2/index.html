Most finance apps ask you to trust their database with years of spending history. Valu takes a different position: the spreadsheet is yours, the account is yours, and the app is just a window into data you already control.

Ownership & privacy

For your eyes only.

Private by design

Your browser talks directly to Google's APIs using your sign-in. Transaction rows are never routed through a Valu server that stores your history.

You own the file

Each group creates a real spreadsheet in your Drive. Open it in Google Sheets, export it, copy it, share it, or delete it. It belongs to you.

Least access

The app only asks for permission to spreadsheets it created — not your whole Drive. That's an API scope choice, not corporate surveillance.

"Only spreadsheets the app creates" describes OAuth permission scope — the smallest footprint we could use. It does not mean a company reads your sheet. The path is: your device → Google → your file.

How it works

Three steps. One spreadsheet.

  1. 1

    Sign in with Google

    You grant the app permission to create and update specific spreadsheets in your account — nothing more.

  2. 2

    A spreadsheet is created for you

    Each group gets a workbook with structured tabs: accounts, expenses, income, balance history, and settings. Ready to use from the app or from Sheets.

  3. 3

    Use Valu every day

    The app reads and writes those tabs through your session. The same numbers appear if you open the spreadsheet directly in Google Sheets.

Features

Built for real households.

Multiple groups, currencies, and the lists you actually need — without losing the spreadsheet underneath.

Groups

Keep personal, household, and side-project finances in separate workspaces, each backed by its own spreadsheet.

Accounts & currencies
