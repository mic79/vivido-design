Ownership & privacy

Older wording like “only spreadsheets the app creates” describes API permission scope—the least access needed—not “the company reads your money.” Here is the clearer picture:

You own your data

Each group is a spreadsheet in your Google Drive. Open it in Sheets, export, copy, or delete it—like any file you control.

Browser → Google

The PWA talks to Google’s APIs using your sign-in. Your transaction rows are not sent to Valu to be stored as a parallel ledger.

Scoped access

The integration asks only for access to spreadsheets this app created—not your whole Drive—so permission stays minimal.

How it works

Three steps. Your spreadsheet is the source of truth you can inspect anytime.

Sign in with Google

You authorize the app to create and update specific spreadsheets in your account—only what Valu needs to run.

Valu creates your sheet

Each group gets a structured workbook: accounts, expenses, income, balances, settings—ready to use from the app or from Sheets.

Use Valu day to day

The UI reads and writes those tabs through your session. Same numbers you’ll see if you open the file in Google Sheets.
